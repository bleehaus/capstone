this is a work in progress, so bugs exist. if you find a bug not listed below, please email miles with screenshots and a descripton.

  • practice buddy currently recognizes she/her, they/them, and he/him pronouns, but doesn't know how to help you talk about someone with multiple pronouns (like they/them and he/him). practice buddy is working learn about neopronouns.
  • practice buddy takes two turns to realize you've reached 5 points. they say "keep it up! your total score is now 5!" but only says "congratulations! your total score is 5!" after you respond.
  • practice buddy sometimes thinks you are misgendering [name] if your reply includes words that include other pronouns besides [name]'s, such as "history" because it includes "his."
  • practice buddy doesn't notice if you misgender [name] if you use [name]'s pronouns in the same reply.
  • practice buddy may have trouble distinguishing "they" as misgendering someone with he/him or she/her pronouns. if you know someone's pronouns are she/her, calling her they/them pronouns is still misgendering.

info

me

who are you?

practice buddy

i'm an in-progress tool developed by miles "bread" lee for their spring 2021 capstone project. i use html, css, javascript, p5.js, and rivescript.

me

are you a chatbot?

practice buddy

yes. i'm essentially a chatbot. rivescript by noah petherbridge is a script for writing chatbots. i'm programmed specifically to help you to practice using someone's pronouns and remind you when you slip up. it's probably best to talk with a real person, but some don't have that option.

me

how do i talk to you?

practice buddy

you can type your reply in the text box at the bottom of the screen. if you want to talk with me, you can enable your microphone by pressing the mic_off icon on the left sidebar under "enable mic to speak." my speech recogniton isn't 100% accurate and you should probably use earphones because i can sadly hear myself!

what should i say to you?

practice buddy

there's a guide on the left sidebar under the "conversation guide" section. click that to open the panel and learn how to talk to me!

me

when should i practice with you?

practice buddy

has your friend recently asked you to start using different pronouns for them? has a peer recently asked to be called a different name? are you rusty or confused? we've all been there, and we just need some repeated practice.

me

why should i practice with you?

practice buddy

being misgendered hurts, and misgendering someone you care about hurts. as trevor j. sangrey put it, learning someone's pronouns often requires mentally taking them out of one bucket and putting them into another. i'm here to help you make that change.

me

what are other things i can do to practice?

practice buddy

you can list someone's pronouns in your phone contact. you can practice using their pronouns in your head, like "i texted [name] today. [pronoun] is so cool and loves cats." you can ask a friend to listen to you as you talk about this person (that's the idea behind this).

me

can i help?

practice buddy

if you're at all interested in helping or supporting this project in any way, such as giving feedback, testing, reporting bugs, or getting involved with the code or writing, you can email miles!

practice buddy
